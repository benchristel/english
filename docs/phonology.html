Pronunciation

Consonants

The consonant phonemes of English are as follows. Paired sounds are unvoiced/voiced pairs.

Labial Dental Alveolar Post-Alv. Gutteral
Nasal m n
Pre-Occ. bm dn
Plosive p b t d k g
Spirant f v θ ð s ʃ h
Approx. w l r j (w)

These are written with their IPA characters, except for the following:

  • /θ/ is spelled th^th.
  • /ð/ is spelled dh^dh.
  • /ʃ/ is spelled sh^sh.
  • /j/ is spelled y^y.

When not between two vowels, nn^nn and mm^mm represent pre-occluded n and m. Between two vowels, the same spellings are usually pronounced /n/ and /m/ (some speakers may lengthen them to /n:/ and /m:/).

Vowels

Front Central Back
High i ɪ u
Mid e ɵ o
Low a

/ɪ/ is spelled y^y, and /ɵ/ is spelled eu^eu. The other vowels are spelled with their IPA characters.

Diphthongs

Allowed diphthongs are ai^ai /aɪ/, au^au /aʊ/, and ae^ae /eɪ/. The combination ow^ow could also be viewed as a diphthong /oʊ/.

Allophony

Word-final s^s becomes voiced [z] after a voiced plosive or fricative.

n^n becomes [ŋ] before /k/ or /g/.

Stress

Stress is on the second-to-last syllable.
