Questions

Yes/No Questions

To make a sentence into a yes/no question, add cannQ at the beginning of the sentence.

Yth2SG#CAP aFIN gweldensee_1#MID "You see" → CannQ#CAP yth2SG aFIN gweldensee_1#MID? "Do you see?"

Content Questions

Content questions start with an interrogative pronoun, like pythwhat "what", pywwho "who", or pellaewhere "where".

The interrogative pronoun is usually fronted:

Pywwho#CAP oPAST gansvogive_2 yth2SG ym marthring#DEF nathere_0? "Who gave you that ring?"

A resumptive pronoun owlTRACE can optionally be left in the interrogative pronoun's place:

Pywwho#CAP yth2SG oPAST gansvogive_2 owlTRACE ym marthring#DEF nathere_0? "Who did you give that ring to?"

If there are multiple interrogative pronouns, only the first is fronted:

Pywwho#CAP yth2SG oPAST gansvogive_2 owlTRACE pythwhat? "Who did you give what to?"

Alternatively, the interrogative pronoun(s) can be left in place:

Yth2SG#CAP oPAST gansvogive_2 pywwho pythwhat? "You gave what to who?"

Questions as Noun Phrases

Syntactically, questions are noun phrases. Thus, they can form part of a larger sentence, standing in for a noun.

Gansffagive_2#INF#CAP mi1SG hwedheltale 'aof_1#LEN pywwho yth2SG oPAST gweldsee_1. "Tell me who you saw."

Mi1SG#CAP ysPRES nag goffknow_1#NEG cannQ ev3SG oPAST troturn_1 dho3PL. "I don't know if he turned them."

Pywwho#CAP aFIN tappatouch_1 ym monstone#DEF vrowFUT ladhadie_0. "Who(ever) touches the stone will die."

Questions as Relative Clauses

Since a question is a noun, it can be related to another noun using the appositive construction with tybe_1:

Yn daranking#DEF#CAP dybe_1#LEN pywwho oPAST bidhadwell_0 waron_1#LEN skathboat. "The king who lived on a boat."
