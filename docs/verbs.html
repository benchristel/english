Verb Forms

Verb Forms

Verbs in EnglishLANGNAME have several forms:

  • active participle: This is the unaltered, dictionary form of the verb.
  • passive participle: Formed by suffixing -ynsBLANK#PASS after a consonant, or -nsBLANCA#PASS after a vowel. E.g. gweldsee_1 "seeing" → gweldynssee_1#PASS "being seen"; ladhavodie_0#CAUS "killing" → ladhavonsdie_0#CAUS#PASS "being killed".
  • middle participle: Formed by suffixing -nBLANK#MID. E.g. gweldsee_1 "seeing (something) → gweldensee_1#MID "seeing (in general)".
  • causative: Formed by suffixing -voBLANK#CAUS. E.g. gweldvosee_1#CAUS "show, make see"
  • infinitive: Formed by suffixing -vaBLANK#INF. E.g. gweldvasee_1#INF "to see (something)". The same form is used for the imperative ("see!"). When applied to a causative, the infinitive affix becomes ^-vva^vva or ^-ffa^ffa: ladhavvadie_0#CAUS#INF "to kill", gansffagive_2#INF "to give".
  • past: Formed by adding oPAST before the verb. E.g. oPAST gweldsee_1 "saw".
  • present: Formed by adding ysPRES before the verb. E.g. ysPRES gweldsee_1 "sees, is seeing".
  • future: Formed by adding vrowFUT before the verb. E.g. vrowFUT gweldsee_1 "will see".
  • finite: A general, "timeless" form. Formed by adding aFIN before the verb. E.g. aFIN gweldsee_1 "sees/saw".

There are several constructions that create a noun from a verb:

  • gerund: formed by suffixing -iodBLANK#GER. E.g. gwelliodsee_1#GER "sight".
  • agentive: formed by suffixing -dirBLANK#AGT. E.g. gansvodirgive_2#AGT "giver", gweldirsee_1#AGT "seer". A patientive form can be created by combining this with the passive: gweldynsdirsee_1#PASS#AGT "seen".
  • resultative: formed by suffixing -gethBLANK#RESULT. E.g. mollgethcurse "curse" ← mollcurse_1, kergethmiddle "middle" ← keramid_1 "amid".
