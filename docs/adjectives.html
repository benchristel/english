Adjectives

Adjectives can be determining or descriptive. A determining adjective is one that narrows down which thing you're talking about. A descriptive adjective just adds additional detail.

Determining Adjectives

Determining adjectives require the noun they modify to have the definite article ynBLANK#DEF before it. Determining adjectives come between the definite article and the noun. The definite article causes mutation on the adjective immediately after it.

  • yn davelevil_0#DEF tarosvanghost "The evil ghost (as opposed to the good ghost)"
  • ym methafinal_0#DEF fonnwish "The last wish"
  • yn gerencentral_0#DEF gwydhtree "The middle tree"

If a noun has multiple determining adjectives, only the first one is mutated.

  • ym methafinal_0#DEF tavelevil_0 tarosvanghost "The last evil ghost"

Some determining adjectives join to their nouns with a hyphen to create a new lexeme; these do not require the definite article. The noun following the adjective is mutated.

  • kerencentral_0-agadhschool#LEN "middle school"

Descriptive Adjectives

Descriptive adjectives follow the noun they modify.

  • tarosvanghost davelevil_0#LEN "An evil ghost"
  • yn dhwegduck#DEF vollynscurse_1#PASS#LEN "the damn duck"

Predicative Adjectives

So far, we've seen adjectives attach to noun phrases as modifiers. Adjectives may also be used as the main predicate of a sentence. This is the difference between the phrase the tall man (modifier) and the sentence the man is tall (predicate).

  • YsPRES#CAP tavelevil_0 yn 'wydhtree#DEF "The tree is evil"
  • YsPRES#CAP mollynscurse_1#PASS yn agadhschool#DEF "The school is cursed"

The word order here is tense-marker, adjective, subject. YsPRES#CAP marks the present tense. Then comes the adjective, then the noun. There is no word in these sentences meaning "to be". In effect, the adjective can double as a verb.

When spoken aloud, ysPRES is often shortened to 's^s. And, for that matter, ynBLANK#DEF is shortened to 'n^n after a vowel. So YsPRES#CAP mollynscurse_1#PASS yn agadhschool#DEF might be pronounced something like 'S^S mollynscurse_1#PASS'n^n agadhschool "The school's cursed".

Comparatives

To express comparisons between things, EnglishLANGNAME uses a periphrasic construction with drowbeyond_1 "beyond":

  • YsPRES#CAP tavelevil_0 yn 'wydhtree#DEF "The tree is evil."
  • YsPRES#CAP tavelevil_0 yn 'wydhtree#DEF drowbeyond_1 yn darosvanghost#DEF. "The tree is more evil than the ghost." (lit. "The tree is evil beyond the ghost")

Superlatives

Superlatives are formed similarly to comparatives, using drowenbeyond_1#MID:

  • YsPRES#CAP tavelevil_0 yn reone#DEF nathere_0#LEN drowenbeyond_1#MID "That one is the most evil."

When drowbeyond_1 or drowenbeyond_1#MID comes immediately after the adjective being put in the comparative/superlative, drowbeyond_1/drowenbeyond_1#MID mutates:

  • Everest^Everest ysPRES tybe_1 yn orodmountain#DEF hirtall_0#LEN dhrowenbeyond_1#MID#LEN. "Everest is the tallest mountain."
  • Spantowchili#PL#CAP tarosvanghost ysPRES tybe_1 yn spantowchili#PL#DEF 'wintasspicy_0#LEN dhrowenbeyond_1#MID#LEN "Ghost peppers are the hottest peppers."
