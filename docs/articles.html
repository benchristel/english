Articles

The definite article (word for the) in EnglishLANGNAME is ynBLANK#DEF, or ym before p, b, or m. It comes before the noun.

The definite article causes the word after it to undergo mutation. Mutation is a grammatical concept unique to EnglishLANGNAME, but don't worryâ€”all it means is that the initial sound of the word changes.

The changes are as follows:

  • p changes to b: penhead "head" → ym benhead#DEF "the head"
  • t changes to d: troiodturn_1#GER "turn" → yn droiodturn_1#GER#DEF "the turn"
  • c and k change to g: cartrevhouse "house" → yn gartrevhouse#DEF "the house"
  • b changes to m: barthring "ring" → ym marthring#DEF "the ring"
  • d changes to dh: dunight "night" → yn dhunight#DEF "the night"
  • g disappears and is represented by an apostrophe in writing: gwydhtree "tree" → yn 'wydhtree#DEF "the tree"

If the initial sound of the word is anything else, it doesn't change:

  • mollgethcurse "curse" → ym mollgethcurse#DEF "the curse"

There is no indefinite article corresponding to a/an in EnglishLANGNAME.

  • gwydhtree "tree" or "a tree"
  • skathboat "boat" or "a boat"
